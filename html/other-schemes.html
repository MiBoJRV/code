Most Common Types of Romance Scams and Our Solutions

  • Stay Informed

    • Keep up-to-date with the latest scam trends and tactics through reputable news sources and consumer protection websites.
    • Educate yourself about common types of scams, their characteristics, and red flags to watch out for.

  • Verify Credentials

    • Research the legitimacy of companies, investment opportunities, or individuals before providing any personal or financial information.
    • Check if the entity is registered and regulated by relevant authorities, such as financial regulatory bodies or government agencies.

  • Protect Personal Information

    • Be cautious when sharing personal details online or over the phone, especially if you didn't initiate the communication.
    • Use strong, unique passwords for online accounts, and enable two-factor authentication whenever possible.

  • Think Before You Click

    • Be skeptical of unsolicited emails, messages, or links, even if they appear to come from reputable sources.
    • Hover over links to preview their URLs before clicking, and avoid downloading attachments from unknown senders

  • Stay Cautious on Social Media

    • Be mindful of the information you share on social media platforms, as scammers can use your personal details for targeted attacks.
    • Don't accept friend requests or engage with unfamiliar accounts that seem suspicious or overly eager to connect.

  • Question Unrealistic Promises

    • If an investment opportunity promises guaranteed high returns with minimal risk, exercise caution. Remember that all investments carry some degree of risk.
    • Avoid any scheme that requires you to recruit others to earn profits, as this could be a pyramid or Ponzi scheme.

  • Secure Your Devices

    • Keep your computer, smartphone, and other devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software.
    • Use secure Wi-Fi networks and avoid conducting sensitive transactions or sharing personal information on public networks

  • Trust Your Instincts

    • If something feels too good to be true or make you uncomfortable, it's likely a scam.
    • Trust your gut and don't hesitate to seek advice from financial experts or professionals.

  • Report Suspected Scams

    • If you encounter a potential scam or fall victim to one, report it to the appropriate authorities, such as the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) or your local law enforcement agency.
    • By reporting scams, you help protect others from falling victim to similar schemes.
