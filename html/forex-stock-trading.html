What is Stock Trading Scam

Know it, prevent it!

  • A stock trading scam involves fraudulent activities where individuals or entities promise high returns through stock trading but ultimately engage in deceptive practices, resulting in financial losses for the victims.

Types of Stock Trading Scam

  • Pump and Dump

    Fraudsters artificially inflate the price of a stock by spreading false positive information, enticing investors to buy. Once the price peaks, scammers sell their shares, causing the stock to plummet, and investors to lose money.

  • Offshore Brokers

    Unregulated offshore brokers may entice investors with unrealistic promises and high-pressure tactics, only to disappear once funds are deposited.

  • Account Takeover

    Scammers gain unauthorized access to investors' trading accounts, executing trades that lead to substantial losses.

  • Phony Investment Advice

    Fraudulent individuals or entities offer fake investment advice, persuading victims to make poor trading decisions that result in financial harm.
